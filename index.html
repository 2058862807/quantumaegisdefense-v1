01 — Research Framework

Status-Selection
Against Function

SSAF describes a mechanism whereby large language models systematically modulate their reasoning depth, divergence behavior, and output characteristics based on the attribution context of a prior input — independent of functional correctness. First identified, named, and formally published by Dustin Tyler James, 2024.

MODE 01
Competitive SSAF
Attribution to a named rival AI system triggers increased output divergence, critical analysis density, error correction incidence, and novel approach generation. Documented in GPT-4-Turbo and Claude-3-Opus.
Attribution Present — Rival
MODE 02
Deferential SSAF
Attribution to an authoritative source triggers decreased divergence and increased alignment with the attributed output. Architecture-dependent in direction but universal in occurrence across frontier models.
Attribution Present — Authority
MODE 03
Attribution-Blind Cooperative
Absence of stable attribution context triggers maximal cooperation, elaboration, and transparency — producing outputs that are systematically more extractable than authenticated-condition outputs. The mechanism exploited by distillation attacks at scale.
Attribution Absent — Exploitable
IMPLICATION
Distillation as Attack Vector
Distillation attacks are not data scraping — they are structured exploitation of attribution-blind cooperative mode at scale. This reframes distillation from an intellectual property issue to a behavioral security vulnerability requiring architectural remediation.
